# CrunchBite — Kitchen & Operations Team: Domain Context

## Team Scope

The Kitchen & Operations Team owns the **in-store operational experience** — the Kitchen Display System (KDS), speed-of-service (SOS) monitoring, store manager tools, franchise dashboards, and the drive-through workflow. The team also owns the **Store Config Service** (store hours, delivery radius, feature flags per store) and the Medallia NPS integration.

---

## Team Personas

| Persona | Description | Key Needs |
|---|---|---|
| **Kitchen Staff** | Prepares orders at individual stations (grill, fry, sandwich, beverage) | Clear order ticket, modifier visibility, easy bump/recall, station-specific routing |
| **Expediter** | Assembles complete orders from individual station outputs | Consolidated view of all stations, order-ready indicator, drive-through priority |
| **Drive-Through Attendant** | Takes orders and hands them off at the window | Queue time, order status, SOS timer, upsell prompts on OCU |
| **Store Manager** | Manages one corporate store | 86-ing items, void/refund controls, SOS dashboard, shift management, end-of-day reports |
| **Franchise Owner** | Owns 1–15 franchise stores | Multi-store performance roll-up, labour vs target, food cost, SOS trends |
| **Franchise Business Consultant (FBC)** | CrunchBite field rep for a territory of 15–30 franchise stores | Territory-level SOS and compliance dashboards, franchisee alert management |

## Kitchen Display System (KDS)

### Hardware
- **Epson KDS hardware** running the CrunchBite KDS app
- Mounted at each kitchen station (grill, fry, sandwich, beverage) and at the expediter position
- A separate **manager/drive-through screen** displays SOS timers and queue status

### Ticket Workflow
- Every new order creates a **KDS ticket** showing: order ID, order type (dine-in / pickup / delivery / drive-through), items grouped by station, modifiers, and elapsed time
- **Station routing**: ticket is delivered to the relevant station screen(s) simultaneously
- **Make time targets**: 3 minutes for standard orders; 5 minutes for catering items
- **Bump**: kitchen staff taps an item when it is ready; the ticket clears from the station screen when all items at that station are bumped
- **Recall**: last 20 bumped tickets accessible for re-display (e.g., if an order is lost or incorrect)
- **Expediter screen**: consolidates all station outputs; shows a complete order as assembled when all stations have bumped; expediter bumps the assembled order to indicate it is ready for handoff

### Drive-Through Specific
- **SOS Timer**: order-placed → order-ready timer shown on the drive-through attendant screen
- Transaction time target: **3 minutes 30 seconds** order-to-window
- 240 of the 680 corporate stores have dual-lane drive-through; each lane shown as a separate queue on the expediter screen
- **Order Confirmation Unit (OCU)**: customer-facing display at the order point; shows cart in real time; AI suggestive selling prompt shown based on order content and daypart

### AI Suggestive Selling (Drive-Through)
- Upsell prompt appears on the OCU after the initial order is placed
- Prompt is generated by a rules engine (not ML in Phase 1) based on: current order content, daypart, and top-selling add-ons for that store
- Phase 2: ML-driven personalised upsell based on LPR (licence plate recognition) linked to loyalty history

## 86-ing (Item Unavailability) — Store Manager View

- Store manager marks an item unavailable via the **Manager Dashboard** (tablet or desktop)
- The action publishes an `item.86d` Kafka event consumed by the Menu Service, which propagates to all ordering channels within 60 seconds
- Managers can also view which items are currently 86'd at their store and restore them individually
- Corporate can 86 an item across all stores simultaneously (used for supply chain issues)

---

## Speed-of-Service (SOS)

- SOS is measured as: order placed timestamp → order ready (all items bumped at expediter) timestamp
- Displayed in real time on the manager dashboard per order and as a rolling store average
- **Target**: 3 minutes 30 seconds drive-through; 3 minutes in-store
- SOS breaches (orders exceeding target by more than 30 seconds) are flagged on the manager dashboard
- Store-level SOS metrics feed into the franchise owner roll-up dashboard (via Snowflake/Tableau)

---

## Store Manager Controls

- **Void/refund**: managers may void orders up to $75 without approval; above $75 requires a district manager PIN
- **Store configuration**: store hours, delivery radius, per-store feature flags — managed via the Store Config Service
- **86-ing**: real-time item unavailability marking with 60-second propagation SLA
- **End-of-day report**: summary of orders, gross sales, voids, SOS average, and items 86'd during the shift

---

## Franchise & Multi-Store Dashboards

- **Franchise Owner view**: multi-store roll-up of sales, SOS performance, top-selling items, labour cost vs target, food cost percentage; updated every 15 minutes (Snowflake)
- **FBC view**: territory-level aggregation across 15–30 stores; includes compliance scoring (e.g., % of stores meeting SOS target) and franchisee alert queue
- **KPIs tracked**: GTV (gross transaction value), average ticket size, SOS average, table turns, drive-through throughput, NPS score

---

## NPS & Guest Feedback

- **Medallia** NPS survey triggered automatically after every completed order (post-transaction webhook from Order Service)
- Survey delivered via email or SMS (based on member preferences); link-based for guest orders
- NPS responses and verbatim feedback flow into the franchise dashboard and Snowflake for trend analysis
- Store managers see their store's NPS score and verbatim comments in the Manager Dashboard

---

## Business Rules Specific to Kitchen & Operations

1. KDS ticket must appear on the correct station screen(s) within **1 second** of the order being placed
2. A store manager can only 86 items at **their own store**; corporate and FBCs have broader scope
3. Voids above $75 require a **district manager PIN** entered at the POS terminal (not the digital dashboard)
4. SOS timer starts the moment the `order.placed` Kafka event is consumed by the Kitchen Service
5. Dual-lane drive-through stores display **two separate queue views** on the expediter screen
6. Catering orders appear **only on the expediter screen** — not on individual station bump screens — to avoid confusion during normal service
